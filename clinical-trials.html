Our Standards

Our Approach to
Clinical Research

Every clinical trial at Makana Health is guided by our unwavering commitment to the highest ethical and scientific standards.

Patient Safety

The safety and well-being of trial participants is our highest priority. Every protocol incorporates robust monitoring, independent data safety monitoring boards, and comprehensive adverse event reporting systems.

Scientific Rigor

Our trials are designed using the highest standards of clinical research methodology, including randomized, controlled designs, biostatistical precision, and peer-reviewed endpoint selection to ensure reliable, reproducible results.

Diversity & Inclusion

We are committed to ensuring our clinical trials reflect the diverse populations who will ultimately benefit from our medicines. Our enrollment strategies actively target underrepresented communities and geographic diversity.

Transparency

We register all clinical trials on public registries, share results regardless of outcome, and publish in peer-reviewed journals. Our commitment to open science builds trust and advances the entire field of medicine.

Participant Guide

For Trial Participants

Understanding the clinical trial process can help you make informed decisions about participation. Here is what you can expect at each stage.

01

Screening

The screening phase determines whether you meet the specific criteria for a trial. This includes a thorough review of your medical history, physical examination, and diagnostic testing.

  • Medical history review
  • Physical examination & lab work
  • Informed consent discussion
  • Eligibility confirmation
02

Treatment

During the treatment phase, you receive the study medication according to the trial protocol. Our research teams monitor your health closely throughout this period.

  • Regular study visits & assessments
  • Continuous safety monitoring
  • Dedicated research team support
  • 24/7 emergency contact available
03

Follow-up

After treatment, follow-up visits ensure we capture long-term data about how you responded. This phase is critical for understanding the lasting benefits and safety of the therapy.

  • Scheduled follow-up visits
  • Long-term outcome monitoring
  • Transition of care guidance
  • Access to study results

Frequently Asked Questions

A clinical trial is a carefully designed research study conducted with volunteer participants to evaluate the safety and effectiveness of new medical treatments, drugs, or devices. Clinical trials are essential for bringing new therapies to patients and are conducted according to strict regulatory and ethical guidelines established by health authorities worldwide.

Each clinical trial has specific eligibility criteria that define who can participate. These criteria may include age, gender, type and stage of disease, previous treatment history, and other medical conditions. Eligibility criteria ensure the safety of participants and the scientific validity of the study. Your doctor or our research team can help determine if a specific trial is appropriate for you.

Patient safety is our top priority. Every clinical trial undergoes rigorous review by independent ethics committees and institutional review boards before it begins. During the trial, an independent Data Safety Monitoring Board continuously reviews safety data. You will be closely monitored by our research teams, and you can withdraw from a trial at any time for any reason without affecting your standard medical care.

Phase I studies test a new treatment in a small group of people to evaluate safety, dosage range, and side effects. Phase II studies expand to a larger group to assess effectiveness and further evaluate safety. Phase III studies involve large-scale testing to confirm effectiveness, monitor side effects, and compare with existing treatments. Phase IV studies are conducted after approval to gather additional long-term safety and effectiveness data in real-world settings.

In most Makana Health clinical trials, the study medication and study-related procedures are provided at no cost to participants. Some trials may also provide compensation for travel, time, and related expenses. Full details about costs, if any, and compensation will be discussed with you during the informed consent process before you decide to participate.
