Disease Understanding

Targeting the Brain's
Most Complex Challenges

Neurological diseases affect over one billion people worldwide, representing some of the most significant unmet medical needs. Our research spans the full spectrum of neurodegenerative and neuroinflammatory conditions.

Neurodegenerative

Alzheimer's Disease

A progressive neurodegenerative disorder causing memory loss, cognitive decline, and behavioral changes. Alzheimer's is the most common cause of dementia, accounting for 60-80% of all cases.

Prevalence

55 million people living with dementia globally, projected to reach 139 million by 2050

Unmet Needs

Disease-modifying treatments that slow or halt progression remain critically limited; current therapies only address symptoms

Current Landscape

Cholinesterase inhibitors and memantine for symptomatic relief; emerging anti-amyloid antibodies represent a new paradigm

View our approach
Autoimmune

Multiple Sclerosis

A chronic autoimmune disease that attacks the myelin sheath of nerve fibers, disrupting communication between the brain and body. MS can cause a range of debilitating physical and cognitive symptoms.

Prevalence

2.8 million people affected worldwide; most commonly diagnosed between ages 20-40, with a 3:1 female-to-male ratio

Unmet Needs

Limited options for progressive MS; need for remyelination therapies that can repair nerve damage rather than just slow progression

Current Landscape

Over 20 FDA-approved disease-modifying therapies for relapsing MS; far fewer options for progressive forms of the disease

View our approach
Neurodegenerative

Parkinson's Disease

A progressive neurodegenerative disorder caused by the loss of dopamine-producing neurons in the substantia nigra. Characterized by motor symptoms including tremor, rigidity, and bradykinesia, as well as non-motor symptoms.

Prevalence

10 million people worldwide; second most common neurodegenerative disease after Alzheimer's

Unmet Needs

No disease-modifying therapies exist; need for treatments targeting underlying neurodegeneration and alpha-synuclein pathology

Current Landscape

Levodopa and dopamine agonists provide symptomatic motor relief; deep brain stimulation for advanced disease; no neuroprotective agents approved

View our approach
Neurological

Migraine & Pain

Migraine is a complex neurological condition characterized by recurrent episodes of severe headache, often accompanied by nausea, sensitivity to light and sound, and neurological aura. Chronic pain syndromes further compound disease burden.

Prevalence

1 billion people globally; migraine is the 2nd leading cause of years lived with disability worldwide

Unmet Needs

Many patients fail or cannot tolerate first-line therapies; need for non-addictive targeted pain treatments with fewer side effects

Current Landscape

CGRP-targeting antibodies and gepants have transformed prevention; triptans remain acute treatment mainstay; significant gaps in chronic migraine care

View our approach

Approved FDA Approved 2024

Neuravox®

(cognivant) tablets, 25 mg / 50 mg / 100 mg

Full Prescribing Information

Mechanism of Action

Neuravox is a first-in-class, novel NMDA receptor modulator with dual neuroinflammation-targeting activity. It works through a unique bimodal mechanism:

  • NMDA receptor modulation: Selectively enhances synaptic plasticity and glutamatergic signaling at sub-excitotoxic levels
  • Neuroinflammation inhibition: Reduces microglial activation and IL-1β/TNF-α signaling in the CNS
  • BBB-penetrant design: Engineered for optimal blood-brain barrier penetration with a CNS-to-plasma ratio of 3.2:1

Approved Indications

Mild-to-Moderate Alzheimer's Disease

For the treatment of cognitive decline in adults with mild-to-moderate AD confirmed by amyloid PET or CSF biomarkers

Alzheimer's Disease-Associated Agitation

For the treatment of agitation associated with Alzheimer's disease dementia in adults

Phase III trials ongoing for Parkinson's disease dementia and frontotemporal dementia

Scientific Approach

How We Unlock the Brain

Our neuroscience platform combines four interconnected pillars of innovation, enabling us to overcome the unique challenges of developing medicines for the central nervous system.

Blood-Brain Barrier Penetration

Our proprietary computational chemistry platform designs molecules optimized for CNS penetration. Using AI-driven molecular modeling, we engineer compounds with ideal lipophilicity, molecular weight, and transporter affinity profiles.

AI-Driven Design CNS Optimization 3.2:1 CNS Ratio

Neuroinflammation Targeting

We've identified key microglial activation pathways that drive chronic neuroinflammation. Our approach targets the NLRP3 inflammasome and complement cascade to modulate the innate immune response within the CNS without immunosuppression.

Microglial Biology NLRP3 Pathway Complement

Digital Endpoints

Our digital health platform uses smartphone-based cognitive assessments, wearable sensors, and passive data collection to capture real-time disease progression. These digital biomarkers enable more sensitive and continuous monitoring of treatment effects.

Wearable Sensors Digital Biomarkers Real-Time Data

Biomarker Discovery

Our translational biomarker platform combines plasma-based neurofilament light chain (NfL), p-tau217, GFAP, and advanced PET imaging to enable precision patient selection and real-time pharmacodynamic monitoring across all our neuroscience programs.

NfL / p-tau217 PET Imaging Precision Medicine
