Prescribing Information

Complete prescribing information, dosing guidelines, safety data, and medication guides for all Makana Health approved products. For healthcare professionals only.

Ix

IMMUNEXIS®

FDA Approved NDA 214-837

makanaximab-vkrt | IL-31 Receptor Antagonist | 150 mg/mL Solution for Injection

1. Indications & Usage

IMMUNEXIS is an interleukin-31 receptor antagonist indicated for the treatment of moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis in adults and pediatric patients aged 6 years and older whose disease is not adequately controlled with topical prescription therapies or when those therapies are not advisable.

IMMUNEXIS can be used with or without topical corticosteroids. Avoid use with other immunosuppressant agents.

Limitation of Use: IMMUNEXIS has not been studied and is not recommended for use in combination with other biologic immunomodulators.

2. Dosage & Administration

Administer by subcutaneous injection. Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thigh, upper arm). Do not inject into skin that is tender, bruised, erythematous, or indurated.

Patient Population Body Weight Loading Dose Maintenance Dose
Adults (18+ yr) All weights 600 mg (2 x 300 mg) SC on Day 1 300 mg SC every 2 weeks
Adolescents (12-17 yr) ≥ 60 kg 600 mg (2 x 300 mg) SC on Day 1 300 mg SC every 2 weeks
Adolescents (12-17 yr) 30 to < 60 kg 300 mg SC on Day 1 150 mg SC every 2 weeks
Pediatric (6-11 yr) ≥ 30 kg 300 mg SC on Day 1 150 mg SC every 2 weeks
Pediatric (6-11 yr) 15 to < 30 kg 300 mg SC on Day 1 150 mg SC every 4 weeks

Refer to full PI for administration instructions with prefilled syringe and autoinjector devices.

5. Warnings & Precautions

  • Hypersensitivity Reactions: Angioedema, urticaria, and serum sickness-like reactions reported. Discontinue immediately if clinically significant reaction occurs. Do not re-administer.
  • Conjunctivitis & Keratitis: Observed in 8.7% of patients in CLARITY trials. Advise patients to report new onset or worsening eye symptoms. Consider ophthalmological evaluation.
  • Eosinophilic Conditions: Patients with pre-existing helminth infections should be treated before initiating therapy. Monitor for vasculitic rash, worsening pulmonary symptoms, or cardiac complications.
  • Risk of Infection: Do not administer live vaccines during treatment or within 16 weeks after discontinuation. Evaluate patients presenting with sustained fever or signs of systemic infection.
  • Arthralgia: Joint pain reported in 3.2% of patients. Most cases mild to moderate and did not require discontinuation.

6. Adverse Reactions

The most common adverse reactions (≥5% and greater than placebo) in adults from the pooled CLARITY-1 and CLARITY-2 trials (N=1,891):

Adverse Reaction IMMUNEXIS (N=1,264) Placebo (N=627)
Injection site reactions 15.3% 6.8%
Nasopharyngitis 11.2% 8.1%
Conjunctivitis 8.7% 2.1%
Upper respiratory tract infection 7.4% 5.9%
Headache 5.8% 4.2%
Arthralgia 3.2% 1.8%

Immunogenicity: Treatment-emergent anti-drug antibodies detected in 4.6% of patients. Neutralizing antibodies observed in 0.8%. No clinically meaningful impact on efficacy or safety.

Cg

CARDIOGUARD®

FDA Approved BLA 761-294

cerivostatin-mkna | Dual PCSK9/Lp(a) Inhibitor | 140 mg/mL & 280 mg/mL Prefilled Syringe

1. Indications & Usage

CARDIOGUARD is a first-in-class dual PCSK9 and lipoprotein(a) inhibitor indicated:

  • As an adjunct to diet and maximally tolerated statin therapy for the treatment of adults with heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (HeFH) or clinical atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) who require additional lowering of LDL-C and/or Lp(a).
  • To reduce the risk of myocardial infarction, stroke, and coronary revascularization in adults with established ASCVD and elevated Lp(a) (≥50 mg/dL).

Limitation of Use: The effect of CARDIOGUARD on cardiovascular morbidity and mortality in patients without established ASCVD has not been determined. CARDIOGUARD has not been studied in patients with homozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (HoFH).

2. Dosage & Administration

Administer by subcutaneous injection into the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Obtain baseline LDL-C and Lp(a) levels before initiation.

Indication Starting Dose Titration Maximum Dose
HeFH / ASCVD (LDL-C primary goal) 140 mg SC every 2 weeks or 420 mg SC monthly Assess LDL-C at 4-8 weeks; if LDL-C not at goal, increase to 280 mg Q2W 280 mg every 2 weeks
ASCVD + elevated Lp(a) (≥50 mg/dL) 280 mg SC every 2 weeks Assess Lp(a) at 12 weeks; continue if ≥25% reduction achieved 280 mg every 2 weeks
Combined LDL-C + Lp(a) reduction 280 mg SC every 2 weeks Assess both markers at 8-12 weeks 280 mg every 2 weeks

CARDIOGUARD should be used in conjunction with maximally tolerated statin therapy. Refrigerate prefilled syringes at 2-8°C. Allow to reach room temperature (20-25°C) for 30-45 minutes before administration.

5. Warnings & Precautions

  • Hypersensitivity Reactions: Hypersensitivity reactions including pruritus, rash, urticaria, and rare cases of anaphylaxis reported. Discontinue if serious reaction occurs.
  • Hepatotoxicity: ALT/AST elevations >3x ULN observed in 1.4% of patients (vs. 0.6% placebo). Monitor liver function tests at baseline, 12 weeks, and periodically thereafter. Discontinue if persistent elevations occur.
  • Myalgia/Rhabdomyolysis: Risk may be increased when used concomitantly with high-dose statins. Advise patients to promptly report unexplained muscle pain, tenderness, or weakness, particularly if accompanied by malaise or fever. Obtain CK levels if myopathy suspected.
  • Neurocognitive Events: Cognitive impairment (e.g., memory loss, confusion) reported in 1.1% of patients. Long-term neurocognitive effects not fully established. Consider assessment if symptoms develop.
  • Pregnancy: Based on mechanism of action, CARDIOGUARD may cause fetal harm. Advise females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception during treatment and for 5 months after the last dose.

6. Adverse Reactions

The most common adverse reactions (≥3% and greater than placebo) from the AEGIS-CV trial (N=8,242):

Adverse Reaction CARDIOGUARD (N=4,121) Placebo (N=4,121)
Injection site reactions 9.8% 5.1%
Nasopharyngitis 6.2% 5.4%
Myalgia 5.1% 3.7%
Influenza-like illness 4.3% 3.1%
Back pain 3.6% 2.8%
Urinary tract infection 3.2% 2.5%

In the AEGIS-CV trial (median follow-up 3.4 years), CARDIOGUARD demonstrated a 34% relative risk reduction in MACE (HR 0.66; 95% CI 0.58-0.75; p<0.001) compared to placebo, both on background statin therapy. Lp(a) was reduced by a median of 72% from baseline.

Drug Interactions Quick Reference

Summary of clinically significant drug interactions for Makana products

IMMUNEXIS Drug Interactions

Avoid

Live Vaccines

Avoid concurrent use. Live vaccines should not be given during treatment or within 16 weeks after discontinuation. Non-live vaccines may be administered.

Caution

CYP450 Substrates (Narrow Therapeutic Index)

IL-31 inhibition may normalize CYP450 activity. Monitor warfarin, theophylline, and cyclosporine levels upon initiation or discontinuation. Dose adjustments may be needed.

Caution

Other Immunosuppressants

Not studied in combination with other systemic immunosuppressants (e.g., cyclosporine, methotrexate, azathioprine). Increased immunosuppression risk. Use not recommended.

CARDIOGUARD Drug Interactions

Monitor

High-Dose Statins (Atorvastatin ≥80 mg, Rosuvastatin ≥40 mg)

Increased risk of myalgia and rhabdomyolysis. Monitor CK levels. Consider dose reduction of statin if CK exceeds 5x ULN.

Monitor

Warfarin & Direct Oral Anticoagulants

No direct PK interaction identified, but patients on anticoagulants should have routine INR/anti-Xa monitoring maintained. Lp(a) reduction may independently affect thrombotic pathways.

No Interaction

Ezetimibe, Fibrates, PCSK9 mAb Monotherapies

No clinically significant interactions observed. CARDIOGUARD may be co-administered with ezetimibe. Avoid co-administration with other PCSK9-targeting therapies (mechanistic overlap, no additive benefit demonstrated).

Important Safety Information

IMMUNEXIS (makanaximab-vkrt): Contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to makanaximab-vkrt or any excipient. Serious hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis, have been reported. Conjunctivitis and keratitis have been reported; advise patients to report new or worsening eye symptoms. Do not administer live vaccines. Evaluate patients presenting with persistent fever or signs of infection.

CARDIOGUARD (cerivostatin-mkna): Contraindicated in patients with a history of serious hypersensitivity to cerivostatin-mkna or any excipient. Monitor hepatic transaminases at baseline and as clinically indicated. Myalgia and rhabdomyolysis risk increases with concomitant high-dose statin use. Not recommended during pregnancy; females of reproductive potential should use effective contraception.
