Disease Landscape

Understanding Cancer

Our oncology programs address the most significant unmet needs across multiple tumor types, informed by deep molecular understanding.

Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

NSCLC

Non-small cell lung cancer accounts for approximately 85% of all lung cancers, representing the leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide. Despite advances in treatment, most patients with advanced NSCLC will eventually progress on standard therapies.

Prevalence ~2.2M new cases globally per year; most common cancer worldwide
Landscape TKIs, immune checkpoint inhibitors, chemotherapy; increasing focus on biomarker-driven therapy
Unmet Need KRAS-mutant NSCLC with limited targeted options; resistance to first-line therapies; CNS metastases

Breast Cancer

HER2+ / TNBC

Breast cancer remains the most commonly diagnosed cancer among women, with triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) and HER2-positive subtypes presenting distinct treatment challenges. Novel antibody-drug conjugates are transforming the treatment paradigm.

Prevalence ~2.3M new cases annually; most common cancer in women globally
Landscape Endocrine therapy, anti-HER2 agents, CDK4/6 inhibitors, ADCs, immune checkpoint inhibitors for TNBC
Unmet Need Durable responses in metastatic TNBC; overcoming endocrine resistance; brain metastases treatment

Hematologic Malignancies

Blood Cancers

Hematologic malignancies encompass a diverse group of cancers arising from blood-forming tissues, including leukemias, lymphomas, and myelomas. Bispecific antibodies and cell therapies are revolutionizing outcomes for patients with relapsed or refractory disease.

Prevalence ~1.3M new cases per year including NHL, AML, CLL, and multiple myeloma
Landscape BTK inhibitors, bispecific antibodies, CAR-T cell therapy, proteasome inhibitors, immunomodulators
Unmet Need Durable complete remissions; managing treatment-resistant clones; reducing treatment toxicity

Solid Tumors

Tumor-Agnostic

Our tumor-agnostic pipeline programs target molecular alterations that drive cancer across multiple solid tumor types, including colorectal, pancreatic, gastric, and bladder cancers. This biomarker-driven approach enables precision treatment regardless of cancer origin.

Prevalence Millions affected across CRC (~1.9M), pancreatic (~500K), gastric (~1.1M) cancers annually
Landscape Tumor-agnostic approvals emerging; NTRK, RET, BRAF-targeted therapies; MSI-H immunotherapy
Unmet Need Expanded biomarker coverage; therapies for rare mutations; combination strategies for cold tumors

Our Medicines

Bringing transformative treatments to patients with the highest unmet need in oncology.

Oncolys®

(onitresib)

FDA Approved EMA Approved

Mechanism of Action

Oncolys (onitresib) is a highly selective, irreversible small-molecule inhibitor targeting the KRAS G12C oncoprotein. It covalently binds to the mutant cysteine residue in KRAS G12C, locking the protein in its inactive GDP-bound state and blocking downstream RAS/MAPK signaling that drives tumor proliferation and survival.

Approved Indications

  • Locally advanced or metastatic KRAS G12C-mutant NSCLC after at least one prior line of systemic therapy
  • KRAS G12C-mutant advanced colorectal cancer (CRC) in combination with cetuximab after prior fluoropyrimidine-, oxaliplatin-, and irinotecan-based therapy

Dosing

960 mg orally once daily, with or without food. Treatment continued until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity.

Key Trial Data: MAKANA-Lung-301

Overall Response Rate (ORR) 42.6%

95% CI: 35.9% - 49.5% | n=345 evaluable patients

Median PFS 7.1 mo

HR 0.56 (95% CI: 0.45-0.70) vs docetaxel | p < 0.001

Median OS 12.5 mo

HR 0.64 (95% CI: 0.50-0.81) vs docetaxel | p < 0.001

Median Duration of Response 11.1 mo

95% CI: 8.2-16.4 months | 36% of responders ongoing at 18 months

Safety Summary: Most common adverse events (≥20%): diarrhea (62%), nausea (34%), fatigue (26%), arthralgia (22%), hepatotoxicity (21%). Grade ≥3 AEs in 45% of patients. Treatment discontinuation due to AEs: 9.4%.

MKN-4280

Anti-TIGIT + PD-1 Bispecific Antibody

Phase II Immuno-Oncology

Mechanism of Action

MKN-4280 is a first-in-class bispecific antibody simultaneously targeting TIGIT and PD-1 immune checkpoints. By co-engaging both receptors on T cells and NK cells, MKN-4280 delivers enhanced anti-tumor immune activation compared to monotherapy approaches, restoring immune surveillance in immunosuppressive tumor microenvironments.

Clinical Program

  • Phase II trial in 1L metastatic NSCLC (PD-L1 TPS ≥50%) + platinum chemotherapy
  • Phase I/II expansion cohort in recurrent/metastatic head and neck squamous cell carcinoma
  • Biomarker-enriched basket study across multiple solid tumor types

Preliminary Phase I/II Results

Confirmed ORR (NSCLC cohort) 38%

n=52, data cutoff Dec 2025 | Mature data expected Q3 2026

Disease Control Rate 78%

CR + PR + SD at 16 weeks

Grade ≥3 irAEs 14%

Manageable safety profile; no new safety signals identified
