Indication

Moderate-to-Severe Atopic Dermatitis

Dermashield is indicated for the treatment of moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis in adults and adolescents (12 years and older) who are candidates for systemic therapy and whose disease is not adequately controlled with topical prescription therapies.

As an oral formulation, Dermashield offers a convenient alternative to injectable biologics for patients and caregivers managing this chronic condition.

Mechanism of Action

Selective JAK1 Inhibition

Dermashield is an oral selective JAK1 inhibitor with rapid onset. It blocks IL-4, IL-13, IL-31, and TSLP signaling via the JAK1/STAT pathway, addressing multiple drivers of atopic dermatitis inflammation and itch.

JAK1 Selectivity

Selectively inhibits JAK1 over JAK2 and JAK3, targeting key inflammatory cytokines (IL-4, IL-13, IL-31, TSLP) while minimizing effects on hematopoiesis.

Rapid Itch Relief

Blocks IL-31 signaling, the primary pruritogenic cytokine in AD, delivering rapid itch reduction (-65%) as early as Week 2 of treatment.

Oral Convenience

Once-daily oral tablet eliminates the need for injections, improving patient acceptance and adherence compared to injectable biologics.

Warnings & Precautions

CBC Monitoring Required

Monitor complete blood count at baseline, Week 4, Week 12, and then every 12 weeks thereafter. Do not initiate if ANC <1,000/mm³, ALC <500/mm³, or hemoglobin <8 g/dL.

Lipid Panel Monitoring

Assess lipid parameters at baseline and at Week 12 after initiation. Manage patients according to clinical guidelines for cardiovascular risk management.

Live Vaccines Contraindicated

Do not administer live vaccines during treatment with Dermashield. Update all vaccinations in accordance with current guidelines prior to initiating therapy.

Adolescent Patients

Same dose (200 mg QD) for adolescents aged 12+ weighing ≥40 kg. Safety and efficacy not established in children <12 years.

Not recommended in patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C). Dose reduce to 100 mg QD for moderate hepatic impairment.

Important Safety Information

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Dermashield is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to dupixorel citrate or any of its excipients; in patients with active serious infections; with concomitant administration of live vaccines; in patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Serious Infections: Dermashield may increase the risk of serious infections. Do not initiate during active serious infection. Monitor patients closely for signs and symptoms of infection during and after treatment. Interrupt Dermashield if a serious infection develops.
  • Herpes Zoster: Herpes zoster reactivation occurred in 2.1% of patients. Consider varicella zoster vaccination before starting treatment, particularly in elderly patients.
  • Hematologic Abnormalities: Monitor CBC at baseline, Week 4, Week 12, then every 12 weeks. Lymphopenia, neutropenia, and anemia have been reported. Interrupt therapy for confirmed cytopenias below thresholds.
  • Lipid Elevations: Increases in total cholesterol, LDL, and HDL have been observed. Assess lipids at baseline and Week 12. Manage hyperlipidemia according to clinical guidelines.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥5%) were acne (8%), headache (7%), and nausea (5%). Other notable reactions included CPK elevation (4%) and herpes zoster (2.1%).

DRUG INTERACTIONS: Avoid use with other JAK inhibitors, biologic immunomodulators, or potent immunosuppressants. Use caution with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors; dose reduction may be needed. No live vaccines during treatment.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Dermashield require injections?

No. Dermashield is an oral tablet taken once daily. This eliminates the need for injections, which can be an important advantage for patients who are needle-averse or prefer the convenience of oral therapy.

How quickly will patients see improvement in itch?

In the CLEAR-SKIN trial, patients experienced a mean itch reduction of -65% by Week 2. This rapid onset is due to Dermashield's blockade of IL-31, the primary pruritogenic cytokine in atopic dermatitis, providing meaningful itch relief very early in treatment.

What laboratory monitoring is required?

CBC should be monitored at baseline, Week 4, Week 12, and then every 12 weeks. A lipid panel is recommended at baseline and Week 12. More frequent monitoring may be warranted in patients with pre-existing cytopenias or risk factors for hematologic abnormalities.
