Mechanism of Action

Precision RET Kinase Inhibition

Tumorix is a highly selective RET kinase inhibitor designed for exquisite target specificity, CNS penetration, and activity against resistance mutations, enabling durable disease control in RET-driven cancers.

Selective RET Binding

Exquisitely selective for RET kinase with >100x selectivity over other kinases (VEGFR2, KDR). Minimizes off-target toxicity while maximizing on-target efficacy.

CNS Penetration

Designed to cross the blood-brain barrier effectively. Brain-to-plasma ratio of 0.8 enables treatment of CNS metastases, a critical unmet need in RET fusion-positive NSCLC.

Resistance Mutation Coverage

Active against common acquired RET resistance mutations including V804L/M gatekeeper mutations, providing sustained disease control even in pretreated patients.

Warnings & Precautions

Hepatotoxicity

Grade 3-4 ALT elevation in 6% of patients. Monitor LFTs every 2 weeks for the first 3 months, then monthly. Withhold or dose reduce per guidelines.

Hypertension

Monitor blood pressure regularly. Initiate or optimize antihypertensive therapy as needed. Withhold Tumorix for Grade 3 uncontrolled hypertension.

QT Prolongation

Monitor ECG and electrolytes at baseline and periodically during treatment. Avoid concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors that may increase Tumorix exposure.

Dose adjustments required for patients with moderate-to-severe hepatic impairment. Monitor liver function closely.

Renal Impairment

Dose adjustments may be needed for severe renal impairment (CrCl <30 mL/min). No adjustment for mild-to-moderate.

CYP3A4 Interactions

Avoid strong CYP3A4 inhibitors/inducers. If unavoidable, adjust Tumorix dose per prescribing information guidelines.

Important Safety Information

INDICATIONS AND USAGE: Tumorix (selpercatinib) is indicated for the treatment of adult patients with metastatic RET fusion-positive non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) as detected by an FDA-approved test.

CONTRAINDICATIONS: None.

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Hepatotoxicity: Serious hepatic adverse reactions occurred in patients treated with Tumorix. Grade 3-4 ALT elevation reported in 6% of patients. Monitor ALT/AST every 2 weeks during the first 3 months, then monthly thereafter, or as clinically indicated. Withhold, dose reduce, or permanently discontinue based on severity.
  • Hypertension: Hypertension occurred in 23% of patients receiving Tumorix. Grade 3 hypertension occurred in 14%. Do not initiate Tumorix in patients with uncontrolled hypertension. Monitor blood pressure after 1 week, at least monthly thereafter, and as clinically indicated. Initiate or adjust antihypertensive therapy as appropriate.
  • QT Interval Prolongation: Tumorix can cause concentration-dependent QTc interval prolongation. Monitor ECG and electrolytes at baseline and periodically during treatment. Avoid concomitant use with other drugs known to prolong the QTc interval and with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors.
  • Hemorrhagic Events: Serious hemorrhagic events can occur. Permanently discontinue in patients with severe or life-threatening hemorrhage.
  • Hypersensitivity: Hypersensitivity reactions, including fever, rash, and arthralgias, have been reported. If hypersensitivity occurs, withhold Tumorix. If resolved, reinitiate at reduced dose with premedication.
  • Impaired Wound Healing: Withhold Tumorix for at least 7 days prior to elective surgery. Do not administer for at least 2 weeks following major surgery and until adequate wound healing.
  • Embryo-Fetal Toxicity: Can cause fetal harm. Advise females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception during treatment and for 1 week after the last dose.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥20%) were dry mouth (28%), hypertension (23%), AST/ALT increase (18%), and fatigue (17%). Grade 3-4 laboratory abnormalities included increased ALT (6%), increased AST (5%), and decreased lymphocytes (4%).

DRUG INTERACTIONS: Avoid concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors or strong CYP3A4 inducers. If concomitant use is unavoidable, adjust Tumorix dose. Avoid concomitant use with drugs that prolong the QTc interval. Tumorix may affect drugs metabolized by CYP2C8 and CYP3A4.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What biomarker testing is required before prescribing Tumorix?

Patients must have RET fusion-positive NSCLC confirmed by an FDA-approved test. Next-generation sequencing (NGS) is the preferred method for identifying RET fusions, including common partners such as KIF5B-RET and CCDC6-RET. FISH and RT-PCR are alternative methods but may not detect all fusion variants. Comprehensive genomic profiling using tissue or liquid biopsy is recommended.

What is the evidence for Tumorix in patients with brain metastases?

Tumorix demonstrated an 81% intracranial overall response rate (ORR) in patients with measurable brain metastases in the PRECISION-RET trial. The drug was specifically designed for CNS penetration with a brain-to-plasma ratio of 0.8. This is particularly relevant as brain metastases occur in approximately 25% of patients with RET fusion-positive NSCLC and represent a significant unmet need.

Does food affect the absorption of Tumorix?

No clinically significant food effect was observed. Tumorix can be administered with or without food. In pharmacokinetic studies, a high-fat meal did not meaningfully alter the Cmax or AUC of selpercatinib. Capsules should be swallowed whole and not crushed, chewed, or opened.
