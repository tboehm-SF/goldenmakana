FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How does spectral flow cytometry differ from conventional flow cytometry?

Conventional flow cytometers use bandpass filters to capture a narrow slice of each fluorochrome's emission spectrum, limiting the number of parameters. The MakaCyte X50 captures the full emission spectrum across 64 detectors and uses AI-powered spectral unmixing to mathematically separate overlapping fluorochromes. This enables the use of fluorochrome combinations with significant spectral overlap that would be impossible with conventional compensation, supporting panels of 50+ parameters.

What are the practical limits for panel complexity on the X50?

The MakaCyte X50 can resolve up to 50 fluorescence parameters simultaneously. In practice, 30-40 color panels are routine with the MakaCyte Panel Builder AI tool, which optimizes fluorochrome selection to minimize spectral spreading error. For panels above 40 colors, careful reagent titration and the use of the autofluorescence extraction feature are recommended. Single-stain reference controls are required for spectral unmixing calibration.

What cell viability can be expected after sorting?

The MakaCyte X50 Sorter achieves >90% post-sort viability for most cell types when using the gentle sort mode with a 100 µm nozzle. Sort purity exceeds 98% across all nozzle sizes. For sensitive cell types such as primary neurons or stem cells, the 130 µm nozzle and reduced sheath pressure settings further improve viability. The integrated biosafety containment system enables safe sorting of clinical and infectious samples.
