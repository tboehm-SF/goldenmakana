Indication

Ulcerative Colitis & Crohn's Disease

Gutrestore is indicated for the treatment of moderate-to-severe ulcerative colitis (UC) and Crohn's disease (CD) in adults who have had an inadequate response, loss of response, or intolerance to conventional therapy or TNF-alpha antagonists.

As a gut-selective agent, Gutrestore provides targeted intestinal immunomodulation without the systemic immunosuppression associated with conventional biologics.

Mechanism of Action

Gut-Selective Immunomodulation

Gutrestore is a gut-selective integrin antagonist targeting alpha-4-beta-7. PEGylated for extended half-life, it blocks lymphocyte trafficking to the gut without systemic immunosuppression, preserving peripheral immune surveillance.

Alpha-4-Beta-7 Blockade

Selectively blocks the alpha-4-beta-7 integrin on gut-homing T lymphocytes, preventing their adhesion to MAdCAM-1 on intestinal endothelium and subsequent tissue infiltration.

Gut Selectivity

Unlike systemic immunosuppressants, Gutrestore acts specifically in the gut, preserving peripheral immune surveillance and reducing the risk of systemic opportunistic infections.

PEGylated Half-Life

PEGylation extends the circulating half-life, enabling every-8-week maintenance dosing after induction and providing sustained therapeutic drug levels between infusions.

Administration Details

Infusion Details

30-minute IV infusion. Observe patients for approximately 1 hour after the first two infusions. No pre-medication routinely required.

SC Self-Injection

108 mg Q2W subcutaneous option after completing IV loading (Weeks 0, 2, 6). Prefilled syringe for home administration. Rotate injection sites.

Assessment of Response

Assess therapeutic benefit by Week 14. If no evidence of response, consider discontinuation. Patients who show response should continue on maintenance therapy.

Corticosteroid Tapering

Once clinical remission is achieved, begin tapering corticosteroids. Do not abruptly discontinue steroids. Taper per standard clinical practice.

Important Safety Information

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Gutrestore is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to vedolixin pegol or any of its excipients; in patients with active severe infections until the infections are controlled.

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Infusion-Related Reactions: Infusion reactions occurred in <1% of patients. If a serious infusion reaction occurs, discontinue the infusion and initiate appropriate medical management. Consider pre-medication for patients with a history of prior infusion reactions.
  • Infections: While Gutrestore is gut-selective and does not cause systemic immunosuppression, patients should be monitored for signs and symptoms of infection. Nasopharyngitis (14%) and upper respiratory infections were the most common infectious events.
  • Clostridium difficile: C. difficile infections were reported in 0.6% of patients. Monitor for new onset or worsening diarrhea. Test and treat C. difficile if suspected.
  • Hepatotoxicity: Hepatic events including elevated transaminases and hepatitis have been reported rarely. Monitor hepatic function if clinically indicated.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥5%) were nasopharyngitis (14%), headache (11%), arthralgia (8%), and nausea (6%). Infusion reactions occurred in <1% of patients. No cases of PML were observed in over 15,000 patient-years of exposure.

DRUG INTERACTIONS: No formal drug interaction studies have been conducted. Use caution with concomitant immunosuppressants (e.g., natalizumab, TNF-alpha antagonists). Concomitant use with aminosalicylates, corticosteroids, and immunomodulators (azathioprine, 6-MP, MTX) is acceptable.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a risk of PML with Gutrestore?

No cases of progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML) have been observed in over 15,000 patient-years of Gutrestore exposure. This is attributable to its gut-selective mechanism: Gutrestore specifically targets alpha-4-beta-7 integrin, which is expressed on gut-homing lymphocytes, not on lymphocytes that traffic to the brain.

Can patients switch from IV to subcutaneous administration?

Yes. After completing the IV induction phase (300 mg at Weeks 0, 2, and 6), patients may switch to subcutaneous maintenance dosing at 108 mg every 2 weeks. This provides a convenient home-administration option for patients who prefer to avoid ongoing infusion center visits.

Is Gutrestore approved for both UC and Crohn's disease?

Yes. Gutrestore is approved for the treatment of both moderate-to-severe ulcerative colitis and moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease in adults. The HARMONY-IBD Phase 3 program included separate cohorts for UC (58% remission at Week 52) and CD (42% endoscopic remission at Week 52).
