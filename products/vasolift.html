Indication

Treatment-Resistant Hypertension

Vasolift is indicated for the treatment of hypertension in adults whose blood pressure is not adequately controlled with a regimen of three antihypertensive medications of different classes, including a diuretic, at optimal doses.

Vasolift should be added to existing antihypertensive therapy and is not recommended as first-line treatment due to its safety monitoring requirements.

Mechanism of Action

Dual Endothelin Receptor Antagonism

Vasolift selectively targets both ETA and ETB receptors, blocking endothelin-1 mediated vasoconstriction and promoting endothelial nitric oxide release. The novel sodium salt formulation ensures enhanced oral bioavailability.

ETA Receptor Blockade

Blocks the ETA receptor on vascular smooth muscle cells, directly inhibiting endothelin-1 mediated vasoconstriction and reducing peripheral vascular resistance.

ETB-Mediated NO Release

Modulates ETB receptor activity on endothelial cells, promoting nitric oxide release for additional vasodilation and improved endothelial function beyond pure antagonism.

Sodium Salt Formulation

The novel sodium salt delivers enhanced oral bioavailability with consistent absorption kinetics, enabling reliable once-daily dosing and predictable pharmacokinetic profiles.

Warnings & Precautions

Pregnancy Contraindication (Category X)

Vasolift is contraindicated in pregnancy. May cause fetal harm. Pregnancy must be excluded before initiation and prevented during therapy.

Hepatotoxicity Risk

Hepatic monitoring required: ALT/AST monthly for first 6 months, then quarterly. Discontinue if transaminases exceed 3x ULN or with signs of hepatic injury.

Fluid Retention

Peripheral edema and fluid retention may occur. Manage with diuretic dose adjustment. Monitor body weight and clinical signs of fluid overload.

Required Monitoring Schedule

Baseline (Before Initiation)

ALT/AST, bilirubin, pregnancy test (females of reproductive potential), hemoglobin/hematocrit, blood pressure.

Months 1-6 (Monthly)

ALT/AST monthly. Check hemoglobin at months 1 and 3. Assess blood pressure and edema at each visit.

After Month 6 (Quarterly)

ALT/AST quarterly. Hemoglobin quarterly. Continue blood pressure and clinical assessment at regular intervals.

Discontinuation Criteria

Discontinue if ALT/AST >3x ULN, bilirubin ≥2x ULN, signs/symptoms of hepatic injury, or confirmed pregnancy.

Important Safety Information

BOXED WARNING: HEPATOTOXICITY AND PREGNANCY

Vasolift can cause serious hepatotoxicity. Measure hepatic aminotransferases prior to initiation and monthly for the first 6 months, then quarterly thereafter. Vasolift is contraindicated in pregnancy and may cause fetal harm. Exclude pregnancy before initiation.

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Vasolift is contraindicated in pregnancy (Category X); in patients with hypersensitivity to endothapran or any excipients; with concomitant use of cyclosporine A; in patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C) or with baseline aminotransferases >3x ULN.

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Hepatotoxicity: Elevations in aminotransferases (ALT and AST) and hepatic injury have been reported. Measure hepatic aminotransferases prior to initiation and monthly for 6 months, then quarterly. Discontinue if aminotransferases are accompanied by clinical symptoms of hepatic injury or increases in bilirubin ≥2x ULN.
  • Embryo-Fetal Toxicity: Vasolift is likely to cause fetal harm based on mechanism of action. Pregnancy testing is required prior to initiation. Females of reproductive potential must use reliable contraception during treatment and for one month after the last dose.
  • Fluid Retention: Peripheral edema is a known consequence of endothelin receptor antagonism (14%). If clinically significant fluid retention develops, evaluate the cause (e.g., Vasolift or underlying condition) and consider need for treatment adjustment or diuretic addition.
  • Decreased Hemoglobin: Decreases in hemoglobin and hematocrit have been observed (anemia 6%). Check hemoglobin at initiation, at 1 and 3 months, then quarterly. Investigate etiology if clinically significant decrease occurs.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥5%) were peripheral edema (14%), headache (11%), nasal congestion (8%), and anemia (6%).

REMS PROGRAM: Available only through the Vasolift REMS Program. Prescribers must be certified. Patients must be enrolled and acknowledge understanding of risks. Pharmacies must be certified and dispense only to enrolled patients.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Vasolift require a REMS program?

Vasolift requires a REMS program due to the risk of hepatotoxicity and embryo-fetal toxicity. The REMS ensures prescribers are certified and educated on monitoring requirements, patients understand the risks, and pharmacies only dispense to enrolled patients.

How often must liver function be monitored?

ALT/AST must be measured at baseline, then monthly for the first 6 months, and quarterly thereafter. If aminotransferases exceed 3x the upper limit of normal or are accompanied by symptoms, Vasolift should be discontinued.

Can Vasolift be prescribed to women of childbearing potential?

Vasolift is Category X and contraindicated in pregnancy. For females of reproductive potential, pregnancy must be excluded before initiation and reliable contraception used during treatment and for one month after the last dose. Monthly pregnancy tests are recommended.
