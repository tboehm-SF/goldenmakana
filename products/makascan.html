Key Features

Advanced Imaging Meets Intelligent Analysis

MakaScan HCS integrates cutting-edge optics, automation, and artificial intelligence to deliver actionable biological insights from complex cell-based assays.

Dual-Mode Imaging

Seamlessly switch between laser confocal (pinhole-based) and LED widefield illumination. Confocal provides optical sectioning for 3D cultures and thick specimens. Widefield maximizes speed for 2D screening.

AI-Powered Analysis Engine

Deep learning models for cell segmentation, organelle classification, morphology quantification, and phenotype clustering. Pre-trained on millions of cell images across diverse assay types.

Live Cell Environmental Control

Integrated incubation chamber with temperature (RT–42°C), CO2 (0–20%), O2 (1–21%), and humidity control enables time-lapse imaging over days without sample compromise.

Important Information

FOR RESEARCH USE ONLY (RUO): MakaScan HCS is intended for research use only. Not for use in diagnostic procedures.

INTENDED USE: The MakaScan HCS system is designed for automated high-content imaging and analysis of cell-based assays in research laboratory environments. Applications include but are not limited to phenotypic drug screening, toxicology profiling, and cell biology research.

LASER SAFETY: MakaScan HCS contains Class 3B laser sources. Appropriate laser safety precautions must be observed during operation and maintenance. All laser interlocks must remain engaged during normal operation. Refer to the instrument safety guide for detailed laser safety protocols.

ENVIRONMENTAL REQUIREMENTS: Operating temperature 18–25°C, humidity 20–80% non-condensing. The instrument should be placed on a vibration-isolated surface for optimal imaging performance.

Please see the complete MakaScan HCS Technical Manual at MakanaHealth.com/makascan/manual.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What 3D imaging capabilities does MakaScan HCS offer?

MakaScan HCS supports comprehensive 3D imaging through confocal optical sectioning with z-stacks of up to 200 slices. The system can image 3D cell cultures including spheroids, organoids, and thick tissue sections with water immersion objectives up to 60x. The AI analysis engine includes dedicated 3D segmentation algorithms for volumetric quantification, measuring parameters such as spheroid volume, shape, viability gradients, and invasion distance.

Can the AI models be trained on custom datasets?

Yes, the MakaScan Vision AI Engine supports transfer learning and custom model training. While the platform ships with pre-trained models covering common cell types and assays, users can fine-tune these models on their own annotated datasets through an intuitive training interface. The system includes active learning tools that intelligently suggest which images to annotate for maximum model improvement, reducing the annotation burden by up to 80%.

What are the data storage requirements for a typical screening campaign?

Storage requirements depend on imaging mode, resolution, and number of channels. A typical widefield 384-well plate with 5 channels generates approximately 15–20 GB of image data. Confocal z-stack acquisitions are larger, with a full 96-well 3D plate producing 50–100 GB. The integrated 20 TB NAS supports approximately 200–400 widefield screening plates or 100–200 confocal 3D plates. The system also supports direct connection to institutional storage infrastructure via 10 GbE networking for unlimited capacity.
