Indication

Moderate-to-Severe Rheumatoid Arthritis

Immunorel is indicated for the treatment of moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis in adults who have had an inadequate response or intolerance to one or more disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs).

Immunorel may be used as monotherapy or in combination with methotrexate or other conventional DMARDs.

Mechanism of Action

Dual Cytokine Neutralization

Immunorel is a bispecific monoclonal antibody targeting both IL-6 and TNF-alpha simultaneously. Dual cytokine neutralization provides superior inflammation control, while the enhanced Fc region extends half-life for convenient dosing.

IL-6 Neutralization

Binds and neutralizes interleukin-6, a key driver of systemic inflammation, joint destruction, and the acute phase response in rheumatoid arthritis.

TNF-alpha Blockade

Simultaneously targets tumor necrosis factor-alpha, blocking its role in synovial inflammation, pannus formation, and cartilage/bone erosion in affected joints.

Enhanced Fc Region

Engineered Fc region extends the circulating half-life, enabling every-two-week subcutaneous dosing while maintaining consistent therapeutic drug levels.

Warnings & Precautions

TB Screening Required

Test for latent tuberculosis before initiating Immunorel. Do not initiate in patients with active TB. Monitor for signs and symptoms of TB during treatment.

Live Vaccines Contraindicated

Live vaccines should not be given concurrently with Immunorel. Update all vaccinations in agreement with current guidelines prior to initiating therapy.

Hepatic Monitoring

Elevated liver enzymes reported in 5% of patients. Monitor ALT/AST at baseline and periodically during treatment. Dose modification or discontinuation may be required for significant elevations.

Important Safety Information

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Immunorel is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to tolimab-mkna or any of its excipients; in patients with active tuberculosis or other severe active infections; with concomitant administration of live vaccines.

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Serious Infections: Serious infections occurred in 2.8% of patients treated with Immunorel, including pneumonia, cellulitis, herpes zoster, and opportunistic infections. Do not initiate during active infection. Monitor closely and interrupt therapy if serious infection develops.
  • Tuberculosis: Evaluate patients for TB risk factors and test for latent infection prior to initiating therapy. Do not give Immunorel to patients with active TB. Initiate TB treatment prior to Immunorel in patients with latent TB.
  • Hepatotoxicity: Elevated liver enzymes reported in 5% of patients. Monitor ALT/AST at baseline and periodically. If ALT/AST >5x ULN confirmed, discontinue Immunorel.
  • Neutropenia: Neutropenia occurred in 3% of patients. Monitor neutrophil count at baseline, 4-8 weeks, then periodically. Do not initiate with ANC <2,000/mm³.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥5%) were upper respiratory infection (12%), injection site reactions (8%), and elevated liver enzymes (5%). Serious infections occurred in 2.8% of patients.

DRUG INTERACTIONS: Avoid use with other biologic DMARDs or JAK inhibitors. CYP450 substrates with narrow therapeutic index (e.g., warfarin, theophylline) should be monitored as IL-6 inhibition may affect their metabolism.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Immunorel different from other RA biologics?

Immunorel is the first bispecific monoclonal antibody that simultaneously targets both IL-6 and TNF-alpha. This dual mechanism addresses two key inflammatory pathways at once, which demonstrated superior ACR50 response (62%) compared to adalimumab (38%) in the RESOLVE-RA trial.

Is TB screening mandatory before starting Immunorel?

Yes. All patients must be evaluated for tuberculosis risk factors and tested for latent TB infection before initiating Immunorel. Patients with active TB must not receive Immunorel. For patients with latent TB, anti-TB therapy must be initiated before starting Immunorel.

Can Immunorel be used with methotrexate?

Yes. Immunorel may be used as monotherapy or in combination with methotrexate or other conventional DMARDs. No dose adjustment is required when used with concomitant methotrexate. However, Immunorel should not be combined with other biologic DMARDs or JAK inhibitors.
