Indication

HER2-Positive Metastatic Breast Cancer

Oncolys is indicated for the treatment of adult patients with unresectable or metastatic HER2-positive breast cancer who have received one or more prior anti-HER2-based regimens in the metastatic setting.

Oncolys delivers a cytotoxic payload directly to HER2-expressing tumor cells, providing a targeted approach with demonstrated superiority over existing antibody-drug conjugate therapies.

Mechanism of Action

Targeted Payload Delivery

Oncolys is a next-generation antibody-drug conjugate that combines a humanized anti-HER2 antibody with a potent topoisomerase I inhibitor payload via a proprietary cleavable linker, enabling precise delivery of cytotoxic therapy to HER2-expressing tumor cells.

HER2-Targeted Binding

Humanized anti-HER2 monoclonal antibody with enhanced binding affinity to HER2 domain IV. Selective tumor targeting minimizes off-target effects.

Cleavable Linker Technology

Proprietary enzyme-cleavable linker designed for stability in circulation with rapid intracellular release. Drug-to-antibody ratio (DAR) of ~8 for maximum potency.

Topoisomerase I Inhibitor Payload

Novel camptothecin-derived payload that induces DNA damage specifically in HER2-expressing tumor cells. Bystander effect provides activity against heterogeneous tumors.

Renal Impairment

No dose adjustment for mild-to-moderate renal impairment (CrCl 30-89 mL/min). Limited data in severe impairment.

Hepatic Impairment

Reduce to 3.6 mg/kg for moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B). Contraindicated in severe impairment (Child-Pugh C).

ILD Dose Modifications

Permanently discontinue for any grade ILD/pneumonitis. Do not rechallenge after confirmed ILD diagnosis.

Neutropenia/Hepatotoxicity

Dose reduce to 4.4 mg/kg then 3.2 mg/kg for Grade 3-4 neutropenia or Grade 2+ hepatotoxicity. Discontinue if toxicity recurs at lowest dose.

Important Safety Information

BOXED WARNING — INTERSTITIAL LUNG DISEASE: Oncolys can cause interstitial lung disease (ILD), including fatal cases. ILD occurred in 9% of patients, with fatal outcomes in 1.2%. Monitor for signs and symptoms including cough, dyspnea, fever, and new/worsening pulmonary infiltrates. Permanently discontinue in patients diagnosed with ILD of any grade.

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Interstitial Lung Disease/Pneumonitis: ILD, including fatal cases, occurred in 9% of Oncolys-treated patients. Monitor for and promptly investigate respiratory symptoms. Permanently discontinue for Grade 2 or higher ILD. Advise patients to immediately report cough, dyspnea, or fever.
  • Neutropenia: Grade 3-4 neutropenia occurred in 16% of patients. Monitor complete blood counts prior to each dosing cycle. Dose modify for Grade 3-4 events. Febrile neutropenia reported in 3.2% of patients.
  • Hepatotoxicity: Monitor liver function tests before initiation and prior to each cycle. Dose reduce for Grade 2 AST/ALT elevations. Permanently discontinue for Grade 3 or higher hepatotoxicity.
  • Embryo-Fetal Toxicity: Can cause fetal harm. Advise females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception during treatment and for 7 months after the last dose.
  • Nausea and Vomiting: Nausea occurred in 46% of patients. Administer antiemetic prophylaxis as clinically indicated. Manage with standard antiemetic therapy.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥20%) were nausea (46%), fatigue (34%), alopecia (30%), and neutropenia (26%). Serious adverse reactions included ILD/pneumonitis (9%), febrile neutropenia (3.2%), and hepatotoxicity (2.8%).

DRUG INTERACTIONS: Avoid concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors. No clinically significant interactions with trastuzumab or endocrine therapies. Caution with medications that prolong QT interval.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is premedication required before Oncolys infusion?

Antiemetic prophylaxis is recommended prior to each infusion given the 46% incidence of nausea. A combination of a 5-HT3 receptor antagonist and dexamethasone is suggested. Standard infusion reaction premedication (acetaminophen, diphenhydramine) should be administered before the first infusion and considered for subsequent infusions based on clinical judgment.

Does Oncolys have activity in patients with brain metastases?

In the ILLUMINATE trial, patients with stable, treated brain metastases were eligible. Subgroup analysis showed a PFS benefit consistent with the overall population (HR 0.38). An intracranial response rate of 63.3% was observed in a dedicated cohort of patients with active brain metastases, suggesting meaningful CNS penetration of the cytotoxic payload.

How should patients be monitored for ILD during Oncolys treatment?

Monitor patients for signs and symptoms of ILD including cough, dyspnea, fever, and oxygen desaturation at each visit. Obtain baseline CT imaging and repeat promptly if ILD is suspected. Hold Oncolys while investigating suspected ILD. If ILD is confirmed at any grade, permanently discontinue Oncolys. The median time to onset of ILD was 5.5 months (range: 1.2-20.8 months). Educate patients to report any new or worsening respiratory symptoms immediately.
