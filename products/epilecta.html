Indication

Drug-Resistant Focal Epilepsy

Drug-resistant focal epilepsy affects approximately 1 million adults in the United States. Despite the availability of numerous antiepileptic drugs, roughly 30% of patients do not respond adequately to their first two medications.

Epilecta is indicated as adjunctive therapy for the treatment of partial-onset (focal) seizures in adults who have not achieved adequate seizure control with prior antiepileptic therapies.

Mechanism of Action

Dual Mechanism Control

Epilecta provides a novel dual-mechanism approach to seizure control by modulating voltage-gated sodium channels and positively modulating GABA-A receptors. The optimized mesylate salt formulation ensures consistent pharmacokinetic performance.

Sodium Channel Modulation

Enhanced blockade of voltage-gated sodium channels in their persistent (late) current state, reducing neuronal hyperexcitability without affecting normal signaling.

GABA-A Positive Allosteric Modulation

Enhances inhibitory GABAergic transmission by binding at a unique non-benzodiazepine site on GABA-A receptors, increasing chloride conductance.

Mesylate Salt Formulation

Optimized mesylate salt form provides consistent dissolution kinetics and reliable plasma levels, enabling predictable dose-response across the titration range.

How Epilecta Works in Focal Epilepsy

1

Persistent Na+ Block

Selectively inhibits persistent (late) sodium currents in hyperexcitable neurons, reducing seizure initiation

2

GABA Enhancement

Potentiates inhibitory GABA-A receptor signaling through a novel binding site, raising seizure threshold

3

Network Stabilization

Dual mechanism synergistically stabilizes cortical and subcortical neuronal networks, preventing seizure propagation

4

Seizure Freedom

Progressive seizure reduction during titration, with many patients achieving complete seizure freedom at therapeutic doses

Warnings & Precautions

DRESS Syndrome

Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms (DRESS) has been reported. Titrate slowly per recommended schedule. Discontinue immediately if rash with systemic symptoms occurs.

Suicidal Behavior

AEDs increase risk of suicidal thoughts. Monitor all patients for emergence of depression, suicidal ideation, and unusual behavior changes.

CNS Depression

May cause CNS depressant effects including somnolence and dizziness. Caution with other CNS depressants and when operating heavy machinery.

Special Population Dosing

Renal Impairment

Reduce maximum dose by 50% for eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m². No adjustment needed for mild-moderate renal impairment.

Hepatic Impairment

Maximum 200mg/day for moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B). Contraindicated in severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).

Elderly Patients

Slower titration recommended. Start at 12.5mg QD and titrate more cautiously, monitoring for CNS depressant effects.

Drug Interactions

Reduce doses of CYP3A4 substrates. Adjust phenytoin and phenobarbital levels as Epilecta may alter their metabolism. Monitor concomitant AED levels.

Important Safety Information

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Epilecta is contraindicated in patients with hypersensitivity to cenobamate mesylate or any component of this product; in patients with familial short QT syndrome.

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • DRESS (Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms): DRESS, which can be life-threatening, has been reported in patients taking cenobamate who received rapid titration. Do not exceed the recommended titration schedule. Immediately discontinue Epilecta if DRESS is suspected and evaluate promptly.
  • Suicidal Behavior and Ideation: Antiepileptic drugs increase the risk of suicidal thoughts or behavior. Monitor patients for the emergence or worsening of depression, suicidal thoughts or behavior, and/or any unusual changes in mood or behavior.
  • QT Shortening: Epilecta shortens the QT interval in a dose-dependent manner. Use is contraindicated in patients with familial short QT syndrome. Avoid use with other drugs known to shorten the QT interval.
  • CNS Depression: Epilecta may cause CNS depressant adverse reactions including somnolence, dizziness, and fatigue. Caution patients about operating hazardous machinery or driving until they know how Epilecta affects them. Use caution with concomitant CNS depressants.
  • Withdrawal Seizures: As with most antiepileptic drugs, Epilecta should be withdrawn gradually to minimize the risk of increased seizure frequency and status epilepticus.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥10%) were somnolence (24%), dizziness (22%), fatigue (14%), and diplopia (10%). Other adverse reactions included headache (8%), nausea (6%), and balance disorder (5%).

DRUG INTERACTIONS: Epilecta is a moderate inducer of CYP3A4 and CYP2B6. Reduce doses of sensitive CYP3A4 substrates (e.g., midazolam, certain hormonal contraceptives). Adjust phenytoin dose (typically reduce by up to 50%). Monitor phenobarbital levels. Epilecta may decrease the efficacy of hormonal contraceptives; consider alternative or backup contraception.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is slow titration required for Epilecta?

Due to the risk of DRESS (Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms), Epilecta must be titrated slowly following the strict recommended schedule. Starting at 12.5mg and increasing every 2 weeks significantly reduces the incidence of this potentially life-threatening hypersensitivity reaction. Do not exceed the recommended titration rate under any circumstances.

Can Epilecta be used as monotherapy?

Epilecta is currently approved as adjunctive therapy only, meaning it should be added to an existing antiepileptic drug regimen. Monotherapy trials are ongoing to evaluate the efficacy and safety of Epilecta when used alone. Do not discontinue concomitant AEDs when initiating Epilecta without physician guidance.

What if a dose of Epilecta is missed?

If a dose is missed, patients should take it as soon as they remember. However, if it is close to the time for the next scheduled dose, the missed dose should be skipped and the regular dosing schedule resumed. Patients should not take two doses at the same time. The long half-life (50-60 hours) provides some buffer for occasional missed doses.
