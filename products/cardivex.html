Indication

Chronic Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction

Cardivex is indicated to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in adult patients with chronic heart failure. Benefits are most clearly evident in patients with left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) below normal.

Cardivex is usually administered in conjunction with other heart failure therapies, in place of an ACE inhibitor or other ARB.

Mechanism of Action

Dual Pathway Inhibition

Cardivex provides dual inhibition of angiotensin II receptor (AT1) and neprilysin enzyme, increasing beneficial natriuretic peptides while blocking harmful RAAS activation. The unique besylate salt improves bioavailability compared to earlier formulations.

AT1 Receptor Blockade

Blocks the angiotensin II type 1 receptor, preventing vasoconstriction, sodium retention, and cardiac remodeling driven by the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system.

Neprilysin Inhibition

Inhibits the neprilysin enzyme, increasing levels of beneficial natriuretic peptides (ANP, BNP, CNP) that promote vasodilation, natriuresis, and reduced cardiac fibrosis.

Besylate Salt Advantage

The unique besylate salt formulation delivers enhanced oral bioavailability, providing more consistent plasma levels and reliable therapeutic effect across diverse patient populations.

Warnings & Precautions

ACE Inhibitor Contraindication

Contraindicated with ACE inhibitors. A 36-hour washout period is required when switching from an ACE inhibitor to Cardivex.

Angioedema Risk

Angioedema occurred in 0.5% of patients. Higher risk in patients with prior ACE inhibitor-related angioedema. Discontinue immediately if angioedema occurs.

Renal Function Monitoring

Serious renal failure occurred in 1.2% of patients. Monitor renal function periodically. No dose adjustment needed for mild-moderate hepatic impairment.

Special Population Dosing

Renal Impairment

No adjustment for mild-moderate (eGFR 30-90). Start 24/26 mg BID for severe (eGFR <30). Not recommended for dialysis patients.

Hepatic Impairment

No adjustment for mild-moderate (Child-Pugh A/B). Contraindicated in severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).

Elderly Patients

No specific dose adjustment for age alone. Titrate based on tolerability, paying close attention to blood pressure and renal function.

Switching from ACE Inhibitor

Mandatory 36-hour washout period when switching from an ACE inhibitor. May switch from ARBs without washout.

Important Safety Information

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Cardivex is contraindicated in patients with hypersensitivity to any component of this product; in patients with a history of angioedema related to previous ACE inhibitor or ARB therapy; with concomitant use of ACE inhibitors (do not administer within 36 hours of switching from or to an ACE inhibitor); with concomitant use of aliskiren in patients with diabetes; in patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Angioedema: Angioedema has been reported with Cardivex (0.5%). Patients with a prior history of angioedema may be at increased risk. If angioedema occurs, discontinue Cardivex immediately, provide appropriate therapy, and monitor until signs and symptoms have resolved.
  • Hypotension: Cardivex lowers blood pressure and may cause symptomatic hypotension (18%). Patients with an activated renin-angiotensin system are at greater risk. Correct volume or salt depletion prior to initiation or start at a lower dose.
  • Impaired Renal Function: Monitor renal function periodically. Changes in renal function may be anticipated in susceptible individuals. Consider dose adjustment or discontinuation in patients who develop clinically significant decreases in renal function.
  • Hyperkalemia: Monitor serum potassium periodically. Patients at risk include those with renal insufficiency, diabetes, or those taking potassium-sparing diuretics or potassium supplements.

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥5%) were hypotension (18%), hyperkalemia (12%), cough (9%), and renal impairment (7%). Serious adverse reactions included angioedema (0.5%) and renal failure (1.2%).

DRUG INTERACTIONS: Contraindicated with ACE inhibitors (36-hour washout required). Use caution with potassium-sparing diuretics, potassium supplements, or salt substitutes containing potassium. NSAIDs may reduce antihypertensive effect and increase renal risk.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Cardivex be used with an ACE inhibitor?

No. Cardivex is contraindicated with ACE inhibitors. A mandatory 36-hour washout period is required when switching from an ACE inhibitor to Cardivex due to the risk of angioedema. Cardivex may be used in place of an ACE inhibitor or ARB.

What monitoring is required during Cardivex therapy?

Monitor blood pressure, serum potassium, and renal function (serum creatinine/eGFR) periodically, particularly during initiation and titration. More frequent monitoring is recommended in patients with risk factors for hyperkalemia or renal impairment.

How quickly should Cardivex be titrated to the target dose?

The dose should be doubled every 2-4 weeks as tolerated. Starting at 49/51 mg BID, the target dose of 97/103 mg BID can typically be reached within 4-8 weeks. Tolerability (primarily blood pressure) guides the titration speed.
