Indication

Prevention of Chronic and Episodic Migraine

Migrasolv is indicated for the preventive treatment of migraine in adults with chronic migraine (≥15 headache days/month) or episodic migraine (4-14 headache days/month). It provides significant reduction in monthly migraine days.

As an oral gepant with no cardiovascular vasoconstriction, Migrasolv is safe for patients with cardiovascular risk factors, addressing a critical unmet need in migraine prevention where triptans are contraindicated.

Mechanism of Action

CGRP Receptor Antagonism

Migrasolv is an oral CGRP receptor antagonist of the gepant class. It blocks calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) binding at trigeminal nerve terminals, preventing the neurogenic inflammation and vasodilation that drive migraine attacks. With a 72-hour half-life and no cardiovascular vasoconstriction, it offers a fundamentally different approach from triptans.

CGRP Receptor Blockade

Competitively blocks the CGRP receptor at trigeminal nerve terminals, preventing calcitonin gene-related peptide from initiating the neurogenic inflammatory cascade that triggers migraine.

Anti-Inflammatory Effect

Prevents the neurogenic inflammation and meningeal vasodilation that drive migraine pain, reducing sensitization of trigeminal sensory neurons and central pain pathways.

No CV Vasoconstriction

Unlike triptans, Migrasolv does not cause cardiovascular vasoconstriction, making it safe for patients with cardiovascular risk factors, coronary artery disease, or uncontrolled hypertension.

Important Safety Information

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Migrasolv is contraindicated in patients with hypersensitivity to fremanepant disodium or any component of the formulation; in patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS:

  • Hepatotoxicity: Elevations in liver enzymes have been reported. Obtain baseline liver function tests (LFTs) prior to initiation, and monitor at Month 3, Month 6, and every 6 months thereafter. Discontinue if clinically significant liver injury is confirmed.
  • Hepatic Impairment: Reduce dose to 75 mg for patients with moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B). Migrasolv is contraindicated in severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).

ADVERSE REACTIONS: The most common adverse reactions (≥2%) were nausea (4%), upper respiratory infection (3.2%), constipation (3%), and fatigue (2.5%).

CARDIOVASCULAR SAFETY: Migrasolv does not cause cardiovascular vasoconstriction. No cardiovascular contraindications. Safe for use in patients with cardiovascular risk factors, including coronary artery disease and uncontrolled hypertension.

DRUG INTERACTIONS: Avoid concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors. Use caution with moderate CYP3A4 inhibitors. No dose adjustment needed for CYP3A4 inducers.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Migrasolv be used in patients with cardiovascular disease?

Yes. Unlike triptans, Migrasolv does not cause cardiovascular vasoconstriction and has no cardiovascular contraindications. It is safe for use in patients with coronary artery disease, uncontrolled hypertension, and other CV risk factors.

What liver monitoring is required during Migrasolv therapy?

Liver function tests (LFTs) should be obtained at baseline, Month 3, Month 6, and every 6 months thereafter. Discontinue Migrasolv if clinically significant liver enzyme elevations occur. The dose should be reduced to 75 mg for moderate hepatic impairment.

Can Migrasolv be taken with other migraine medications?

Yes, Migrasolv can be used alongside acute migraine treatments including triptans, NSAIDs, and acetaminophen. Avoid concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors. There are no known drug interactions with common acute migraine therapies.
