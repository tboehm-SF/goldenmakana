Important Information

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE: MakaStation One is designed to support workflows in environments governed by 21 CFR Part 11, ISO 15189, and GLP/GMP guidelines. Compliance is dependent on proper configuration, validation, and use according to documented procedures.

INTENDED USE:

  • For Research Use Only (RUO): MakaStation One in its standard configuration is intended for research use only and is not intended for use in diagnostic procedures. Clinical diagnostic configurations require separate IVD validation.
  • Installation Requirements: Professional installation by Makana Health certified field engineers is required. Site must meet specified environmental conditions (18–28°C, 20–80% RH) and provide adequate power, ventilation, and floor loading capacity.
  • Operator Training: All operators must complete Makana Health operator training and certification prior to unsupervised use. Annual recertification is recommended to maintain compliance and operational proficiency.

DATA INTEGRITY: MakaOrchestrator software includes electronic signatures, audit trails, and user access controls to support 21 CFR Part 11 compliance. Regular software updates and database backups are recommended.

SAFETY: MakaStation One includes safety interlocks, light curtains, and emergency stop mechanisms. All safety features must remain enabled during operation. Consult the safety manual for complete hazard information.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can MakaStation One integrate with third-party instruments?

Yes. MakaStation One features an open architecture built on the SiLA 2 standard protocol, enabling integration with a wide range of third-party instruments including liquid handlers, plate readers, thermal cyclers, centrifuges, and sealers from major manufacturers. Our integration team provides custom driver development for instruments not yet in our compatibility library. Contact our applications team for a compatibility assessment of your specific instruments.

Is MakaStation One suitable for cleanroom operation?

MakaStation One can be configured for cleanroom environments up to ISO Class 7 (Class 10,000). The system uses sealed bearings, HEPA-filtered air circulation within the enclosure, and stainless steel surfaces that support standard cleaning and decontamination protocols. Cleanroom-specific configurations include enhanced filtration, positive pressure enclosures, and materials selected for low particle generation. Contact our sales engineering team to discuss your specific cleanroom requirements.

Does the system support remote monitoring and control?

Yes. MakaOrchestrator v5.0 includes a web-based dashboard accessible from any device on your network or via secure VPN. Operators can monitor real-time system status, view run progress, receive alerts for errors or instrument exceptions, and review completed run reports remotely. Push notifications can be configured for critical events such as run completion, error conditions, or reagent depletion. Mobile apps for iOS and Android are available for on-the-go monitoring.
