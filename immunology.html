Disease Understanding

The immune system,
when it turns inward.

Immune-mediated diseases occur when the body's defense system mistakenly attacks its own healthy tissue, causing chronic inflammation and progressive damage across multiple organ systems.

Rheumatoid Arthritis

Musculoskeletal

A chronic autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks joint linings, causing painful swelling that can eventually result in bone erosion, joint deformity, and systemic complications affecting the heart, lungs, and eyes.

~1%

Global Prevalence

18M+

People Worldwide

Unmet Needs

Up to 40% of RA patients do not respond adequately to current biologic therapies. Disease progression continues despite treatment in many cases, and sustained drug-free remission remains elusive for most patients.

Psoriasis & Psoriatic Arthritis

Dermatologic / Joint

An immune-mediated inflammatory disease causing rapid skin cell turnover, resulting in thick, scaly plaques. Up to 30% of psoriasis patients develop psoriatic arthritis, a debilitating joint condition that can cause irreversible damage if untreated.

2-3%

Global Prevalence

125M+

People Worldwide

Unmet Needs

Complete skin clearance (PASI 100) remains difficult to achieve for many patients. Comorbidities including cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and depression are under-managed. Early PsA detection is critical but often delayed.

Inflammatory Bowel Disease

Gastrointestinal

Encompassing Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis, IBD involves chronic inflammation of the gastrointestinal tract. These conditions cause severe abdominal pain, persistent diarrhea, fatigue, and malnutrition, with significant risk of surgical intervention.

6.8M+

People Worldwide

Rising

Global Incidence

Unmet Needs

Up to 50% of patients lose response to biologic therapies over time. Mucosal healing and endoscopic remission remain primary goals. New approaches targeting multiple inflammatory pathways are urgently needed to improve long-term outcomes.

Lupus (SLE)

Systemic Autoimmune

Systemic lupus erythematosus is a complex autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks tissues throughout the body, including joints, skin, kidneys, blood cells, brain, heart, and lungs. It disproportionately affects women of childbearing age.

5M+

People Worldwide

9:1

Female:Male Ratio

Unmet Needs

No new lupus therapies were approved for over 50 years until recently. Lupus nephritis threatens kidney failure in up to 60% of patients. Reducing steroid dependence and preventing organ damage remain critical unmet goals.

Approved Therapies

Our immunology medicines.

Two approved therapies delivering meaningful outcomes for patients with immune-mediated diseases, backed by robust clinical evidence.

FDA Approved

Immunorel®

(talirumab) injection, for subcutaneous use

Immunorel is a first-in-class humanized monoclonal antibody that selectively targets the IL-17A/F and IL-23 dual pathway, providing comprehensive control of the inflammatory cascade driving multiple immune-mediated conditions.

Mechanism of Action

1

Binds with high affinity to IL-17A and IL-17F cytokines

2

Inhibits IL-23 receptor signaling on Th17 cells

3

Disrupts the IL-17/IL-23 axis, reducing downstream inflammation

IL-17/IL-23 Pathway

Approved Indications

Moderate-to-severe Plaque Psoriasis Active Psoriatic Arthritis Moderate-to-severe Rheumatoid Arthritis Ankylosing Spondylitis

FDA Approved

Dermashield®

(remisara) cream, 1% / oral tablet, 5mg

Dermashield is a selective TYK2 inhibitor designed for dermatologic autoimmune conditions. Available as a topical cream and oral tablet, it offers flexible treatment options targeting the intracellular signaling pathways that drive skin inflammation.

Mechanism of Action

1

Selectively inhibits TYK2 kinase activity

2

Blocks IL-12, IL-23, and Type I IFN signaling

3

Minimal impact on JAK1/2/3 pathways

Approved Indications

Moderate-to-severe Atopic Dermatitis Chronic Plaque Psoriasis (Topical) Alopecia Areata

Our Scientific Approach

Precision immunology.
Redefining treatment.

Our research strategy combines deep biological understanding with cutting-edge technology to develop therapies that address the root causes of immune dysregulation.

Precision Immunology

Using multi-omic profiling and single-cell analysis to identify patient subpopulations most likely to respond to specific immunomodulatory therapies.

Biomarker-Guided Therapy

Integrating companion diagnostics and predictive biomarkers into clinical development to identify patients most likely to benefit and optimize treatment decisions.

Novel Targets

Exploring next-generation pathways including JAK/STAT, TYK2, IRAK4, and ROR-gamma-t to develop differentiated therapies with improved selectivity and safety profiles.

Next-Gen Biologics

Engineering bispecific antibodies, nanobodies, and antibody-drug conjugates with enhanced tissue penetration, extended half-life, and reduced immunogenicity.
