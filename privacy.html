Introduction & Scope

Who We Are

Makana Health AG ("Makana Health," "we," "us," or "our") is a global pharmaceutical company headquartered in Bern, Switzerland, dedicated to discovering, developing, and delivering innovative medicines that improve and extend people's lives. We operate in more than 60 countries through subsidiaries, affiliates, and partner organizations.

What This Policy Covers

This Privacy Policy explains how Makana Health collects, uses, shares, retains, and protects personal information in connection with:

  • Our websites, mobile applications, and digital platforms (collectively, "Sites")

  • Clinical trials and research studies
  • Healthcare professional (HCP) engagement programs
  • Adverse event and safety reporting
  • Medical information requests
  • Employment and recruitment activities
  • Business-to-business interactions with partners and vendors

How We Use Your Information

Makana Health processes personal information for the following purposes. We only process your data when we have a valid legal basis to do so.

Service Delivery & Account Management

To provide access to our websites, portals, and digital services; manage your account; process registrations for events, webinars, and programs; fulfill requests for samples or educational materials.

Medical Information Requests

To respond to inquiries from patients and healthcare professionals about our products, including providing prescribing information, safety data, and clinical evidence.

Adverse Event Reporting & Pharmacovigilance

To comply with our legal obligations to monitor, collect, assess, and report adverse events and safety information to regulatory authorities worldwide, including the FDA, EMA, and Swissmedic. This processing is based on our legal obligations under pharmaceutical regulations.

Clinical Trial Operations

To manage clinical trial recruitment, enrollment, participation, follow-up, and reporting in accordance with ICH-GCP guidelines and applicable regulatory requirements. Processing of clinical trial participant data is governed by separate informed consent documents.

Marketing Communications

Where you have provided your consent, to send you newsletters, product updates, event invitations, and other promotional materials. You may withdraw your consent at any time by using the unsubscribe link in our emails or contacting us directly.

Website Improvement & Analytics

To analyze how our Sites are used, identify trends, diagnose technical issues, and improve user experience. This may include aggregated and pseudonymized data that does not directly identify you.

Legal Compliance & Fraud Prevention

To comply with applicable laws and regulations; respond to legal process or government requests; enforce our terms and conditions; protect the rights, property, and safety of Makana Health, our employees, and others; detect and prevent fraud or other illegal activities.

International Data Transfers

As a global pharmaceutical company, Makana Health may transfer your personal data to countries outside your country of residence. We ensure that all international data transfers are conducted in compliance with applicable data protection laws by implementing appropriate safeguards.

Standard Contractual Clauses

We use European Commission-approved Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as adopted in June 2021, supplemented by transfer impact assessments where necessary, to govern transfers to countries without adequate data protection levels.

Adequacy Decisions

Where the European Commission or the Swiss Federal Council has determined that a recipient country provides an adequate level of data protection, transfers may be made on the basis of such adequacy decisions.

Binding Corporate Rules

Intra-group transfers within the Makana Health corporate family are governed by our Binding Corporate Rules, which have been approved by our lead supervisory authority and ensure a consistent standard of protection.

EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework

For transfers to the United States, we rely on the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework where our U.S. entities are certified, and may supplement with SCCs as an additional safeguard measure.

You may obtain a copy of the relevant transfer safeguards by contacting our Data Protection Office at privacy@makanahealth.com.

Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data. We are committed to facilitating the exercise of these rights and will respond to valid requests within the legally required timeframes.

Right to Access (GDPR Art. 15)

You have the right to obtain confirmation whether we process your personal data and, if so, to receive a copy of such data along with information about the purposes, categories, recipients, retention periods, and your rights. We provide the first copy free of charge; additional copies may incur a reasonable administrative fee.

Right to Rectification (GDPR Art. 16)

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal data and to have incomplete data completed. We will verify and update the data promptly and notify any recipients to whom the data has been disclosed.

Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") (GDPR Art. 17)

You may request deletion of your personal data when it is no longer necessary, you withdraw consent, or the data has been unlawfully processed. Please note that this right does not apply where processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation (such as pharmacovigilance) or for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

Right to Restrict Processing (GDPR Art. 18)

You may request that we restrict the processing of your personal data while we verify its accuracy, assess a legitimate interest objection, or determine whether our legitimate grounds override yours. During restriction, we will only store the data and process it with your consent or for legal claims.

Right to Data Portability (GDPR Art. 20)

Where processing is based on consent or contract and carried out by automated means, you may request to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (such as CSV or JSON) and have it transmitted directly to another controller where technically feasible.

Right to Object (GDPR Art. 21)

You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. When you object to direct marketing, we will cease processing immediately. For other objections, we will stop unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we rely on your consent for processing, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing that occurred before the withdrawal. You can withdraw consent by contacting us, using unsubscribe links, or adjusting cookie preferences.

CCPA/CPRA Rights for California Residents

If you are a California resident, you also have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected, the sources, purposes, and third parties with whom it was shared in the preceding 12 months.
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information we hold, subject to certain exceptions (e.g., completing transactions, legal compliance, security).
  • Right to Correct: Request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing: Although Makana Health does not sell personal information, you may exercise this right at any time.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Information: Direct us to use sensitive personal information only for purposes authorized under the CPRA.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA/CPRA rights. You will not receive different pricing, quality of service, or be denied services.

To exercise any of these rights, please use the Data Subject Request Form below or contact our Data Protection Office at privacy@makanahealth.com.

Children's Privacy

Our Sites and services are not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16 through our websites or marketing activities. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data from a child under 16 without appropriate parental consent, we will take steps to delete such information promptly.

Clinical Trials Involving Minors

Some of our clinical trials may involve pediatric populations. In such cases, participation is governed by separate informed consent and assent processes that comply with applicable regulatory requirements, including the EU Clinical Trials Regulation (EU) 536/2014 and the U.S. Pediatric Research Equity Act. Parental or guardian consent is always required, and the child's assent is obtained where appropriate based on age and maturity. The Ethics Committee/IRB must specifically approve the inclusion of minors before any data collection begins.

If you believe a child under 16 has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately at privacy@makanahealth.com.

Security Measures

Makana Health implements comprehensive technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security program is aligned with industry standards and undergoes regular independent assessment.

Encryption

All data in transit is protected using TLS 1.3 encryption. Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 encryption across all databases and storage systems. Encryption keys are managed through a dedicated hardware security module (HSM).

Access Controls

We enforce role-based access control (RBAC) with the principle of least privilege. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all employees accessing personal data. Access is regularly reviewed and recertified on a quarterly basis.

Security Audits

Annual penetration testing by independent third parties, continuous vulnerability scanning, and regular internal security audits. We maintain SOC 2 Type II certification, attesting to our security, availability, and confidentiality controls.

Incident Response

We maintain a documented incident response plan with 24/7 monitoring capabilities. In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to your rights, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours and affected individuals without undue delay, as required by GDPR Article 33/34.

While we implement robust security measures, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We continuously review and enhance our security practices to address emerging threats.
